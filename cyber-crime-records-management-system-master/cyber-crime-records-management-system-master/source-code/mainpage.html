Online Awareness

HACKING

Hacking is the unauthorized access or manipulation of computer systems, networks, and data, often for malicious purposes such as data theft, financial gain, or system disruption. As technology evolves, so do the techniques used by hackers, ranging from phishing and malware to sophisticated attacks like Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) and ransomware. Cybersecurity, therefore, becomes essential in protecting sensitive information and maintaining system integrity. Key defenses include using strong, unique passwords, multi-factor authentication (MFA), encryption, and constant monitoring for vulnerabilities. Education on online threats, robust security infrastructures, and rapid response protocols are crucial for individuals and organizations to mitigate the risks of hacking and cybercrime in an increasingly digital world.

It is one of the most common types of financial crime in the digital age, often resulting from data breaches, phishing attacks, or card skimming. Fraudsters may steal card details through hacking, malware, or even by physical theft of the card itself. The rise of online shopping and digital payments has increased the risk of credit card fraud, prompting businesses and individuals to adopt enhanced security measures such as encryption, tokenization, and multi-factor authentication to protect sensitive data. Prevention and rapid detection are critical to minimizing losses and ensuring financial security.

General Tips

Password Protection

Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts. Consider using a password manager to store them securely.

Software Updates

Keep your operating system, browser, and other software up to date to protect against vulnerabilities.

Be Wary of Phishing Scams

Be cautious of emails and messages asking for personal information or urging immediate action.
